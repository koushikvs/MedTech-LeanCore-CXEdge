Core Workflows & Capabilities

Six surgery-context-aware workflows. Plus AI on the roadmap.

Each workflow is built around the realities of surgical scheduling, regulatory traceability and staff under time pressure — making the next-best action obvious without requiring users to interpret raw inventory lists.

📌 AFS · Surgery Readiness

Reserve IOLs for Surgery

Reserve a specific lens for a future surgery date so it cannot be double-booked — filling a critical gap in standard ERP status models.

  • Near-real-time interim status ensures users see the reservation immediately, even before SAP S/4HANA reconciliation completes
  • Supports look-ahead scheduling integration where available — surgeries up to +14 days surface automatically
  • Diopter / model availability check before reservation prevents booking lenses that don't exist on shelf
  • Reservation evidence captured in the activity log for audit
Value: Eliminates day-of-surgery surprises, reduces emergency procurement, and keeps the OR running on schedule.

↩️ RTS · Inventory Accuracy

Return to Shelf

Reverses a reservation when a lens was reserved but not used — marks it back as available for future procedures.

  • Prevents "phantom reservations" from locking inventory permanently
  • Operates within the same near-real-time interim state model as AFS
  • Confidence in inventory accuracy without waiting for ERP sync
  • Each RTS action is logged in the activity log with user, time and reason
Value: Keeps inventory available for future surgeries instead of being trapped in stale reservation states.
🩺 RAI · Post-Surgery

Report as Implanted

Records implant activity post-surgery: surgeon, surgery date, and implanted lenses. Creates implant cards enabling downstream billing reconciliation.

  • Captures structured implant data — surgeon, date, OR, patient context, lens serial
  • Creates implant cards that downstream systems use to reconcile billing & inventory
  • Reduces unbilled-IOL backlog by closing the loop between surgery and billing
  • Supports compliance documentation for implantable device traceability
Value: Closes the surgery-to-billing loop quickly, reduces revenue leakage from unbilled implants, and builds a clean audit trail for regulators.

🚫 RAD · Compliance

Report as Destroyed

Registers a lens as no longer fit for use. Once destroyed, it cannot be used anywhere and is removed from inventory after processing.

  • Multiple entry points: dedicated flow, scan inventory, or expiry trend widget
  • Meeting users at the natural point of decision — no flow switching
  • Reduces compliance risk and prevents accidental usage of non-viable devices
  • Each destroy action captures user, reason and source for full audit trail
Value: Reduces compliance risk and prevents accidental usage of non-viable devices, while keeping audit trails clean.
📡 Hardware Integration

RFID & Scan Inventory

Request RFID scanning hardware through the platform and perform bulk inventory scans to add large volumes of lenses efficiently.

  • Self-service hardware request — order RFID scanners directly through the platform
  • Bulk scan capability for high-volume inventory ingestion
  • Multiple adoption paths — RFID or barcode — so markets at different maturity levels can still realize value
  • Scan history with full event log: who, when, where, status
  • Incremental and bulk updates integrated with classification & transfer logic
Value: Cuts inventory ingestion time, supports global rollouts at varying maturity levels, and builds a complete event log for traceability.

📈 Commercial

IOL Aging Trend & Virtual Consignment

Provides lens age detail required to manage virtual-consignment arrangements where items are billed if unused after a defined period.

  • Manages virtual consignment arrangements with proactive triggers
  • Aging buckets (≤30d, 31–90d, >90d) make billing-trigger exposure visible
  • Pairs with Expiry Trend widget to drive utilization & minimize waste
  • Proactive monitoring before billing triggers occur — prevents surprise charges
Value: Prevents surprise billing exposure under virtual-consignment models, drives utilization, and protects facility margins.
AI Extensions on the Roadmap

From operational signals to anticipatory action.

Today's widgets surface the right data at the right moment. The H2 2026 backlog adds AI features that act on that data — proactively rebalancing, flagging recalls, and answering staff questions in plain language.

📊

Dynamic Par Levels

Replaces static reorder points with AI-tuned thresholds that adjust based on the upcoming surgical schedule — avoiding stockouts and excess inventory simultaneously.

🚨

Recall Lockdown

For lot-specific recalls, AI cross-references inventory and instantly locks affected items so they cannot be scanned out. Flagged items are also surfaced in Commerce for replacement workflows.

🔄

Inventory Recommendation Engine

Analyzes facility holding patterns and proactively recommends transfers between locations to balance utilization — turning surplus at one site into readiness at another.

💬

Intelligent Inventory Search

Natural-language queries like "Do I have enough lenses for next month's surgeries?" answered in-context — staff stop wrestling with filters and just ask.
